Create a high click-through-rate YouTube thumbnail scene for a tech review video.
The image must look like a real photograph captured during filming, not a graphic design or AI render.
The thumbnail should feel authentic, professional, and product-focused, similar to thumbnails created by experienced tech reviewers.
Colors should appear slightly vibrant and saturated for visual pop, while still remaining natural and believable.

CORE PRIORITY
• The product must be the largest and most visually dominant object in the frame
• The viewer’s eye should go product → human reaction
• No fictional elements
• Do not include unrelated objects, props, metaphors, or symbols
• Only include the real product and natural human interaction
The product must clearly be the hero of the frame.

COMPOSITION
Design the frame with a strong left-heavy layout.
• The product must dominate the left side of the frame (25–35% visual weight)
• The product should sit naturally on a desk or table with realistic perspective
• Leave extra empty space above and below the subjects so the image can be safely cropped from the top and bottom later.
• The product must appear sharp, highly detailed, and clearly lit
• Do not center the product
Leave intentional negative space above and to the left of the product.
This empty space should be clean and uncluttered so text or graphics could be added later without covering the product.
Nothing important should occupy this reserved space.

PRODUCT PRESENTATION
• The product must appear physically large and close to the viewer
• Clear material detail (glass reflections, plastic texture, metal edges)
• Subtle highlights should reveal the product’s shape
• Slight rim lighting or soft edge highlight is allowed to separate it from the background
• Keep all lighting realistic and natural
Do not redesign, exaggerate, distort, or stylize the product.
The product should feel like the real retail version sitting on a desk during a review.

HUMAN ELEMENT
Include a realistic tech reviewer on the RIGHT side of the frame.
• The person must appear behind or beside the product, not in front of it
• The face must support the product visually and not compete with it
• Natural skin texture and lighting
• The reviewer should appear slightly smaller in the frame to maintain safe margins for later cropping.
• Slight asymmetry and realistic imperfections
Expression should be subtle and believable:
• thoughtful skepticism
• impressed curiosity
• mild disappointment
• quiet realization
Avoid exaggerated YouTube reaction faces.
The person should interact naturally with the product:
• looking at it
• adjusting it
• pointing toward it
• reacting to it
The human must always be smaller in visual weight than the product.

BACKGROUND
• Realistic tech workspace or desk environment
• Slightly blurred background using natural depth of field
• Background should be slightly darker and less saturated than the product
This ensures the product visually pops.
Avoid:
• dramatic cinematic lighting
• glowing effects
• fantasy lighting
• cluttered environments

CAMERA & REALISM
The scene should feel like it was shot on a real camera indoors.
• The camera framing should feel slightly pulled back, leaving breathing room around the product and person.
Suggested look:
• 35–50mm lens
• shallow depth of field
• soft indoor lighting (window light or desk lighting)
Additional realism details:
• subtle natural shadows
• realistic reflections on product surfaces
• natural skin texture
• no HDR look
• no heavy color grading
• no extreme sharpening

VISUAL HIERARCHY (STRICT)
1️⃣ Product2️⃣ Human reaction
The product must instantly capture attention.
The viewer should immediately understand what product is being reviewed.

GOAL
The thumbnail should feel:
• real
• product-focused
• trustworthy
• opinion-driven
The product is the hero.The human supports the message.
The composition must leave clear space above and to the left of the product so headline graphics can be added later.
