Create a high click-through-rate YouTube thumbnail scene for a tech review video using strong foreground/background depth.
The image must look like a real photo captured during filming — spontaneous, natural, and believable. It must not look like a graphic design poster or an AI render.
FRAME COMPOSITION
Design the image with a left-heavy layout.
The product must appear in the left third of the frame, leaving open space in the upper-left area where headline text could be added later.
LEFT SIDE — FOREGROUND PRODUCT
• Product extremely close to the camera lens
 • Occupies roughly 25–30% of frame width
 • Clearly the sharpest object in the image
 • Held casually in one hand
 • Slight natural tilt allowed
 • Visible material texture and realistic detail
 • Product must separate from background using true depth of field, not glow effects
 • The product should visually outweigh the hand, but not dominate the entire frame
 • The product should sit slightly below the vertical center of the frame
 • The product must feel physically closer to the viewer than the person behind it
RIGHT SIDE — BACKGROUND HUMAN
• Person positioned behind the product on the right side
 • Face softly blurred due to shallow depth of field
 • Person pointing toward the product
 • Expression subtle and believable:
 quiet realization, mild regret, thoughtful skepticism, or impressed surprise
 • No exaggerated YouTube face
 • Lighting must match the product and feel natural
 • The human should support the product visually and not compete with it
CAMERA & REALISM
The image should feel like it was shot on a 50mm lens at f/1.8–f/2.8 indoors.
• Realistic shallow depth of field
 • Natural indoor lighting (window light or soft room light)
 • Slight natural shadows
 • Natural skin texture and small imperfections
 • No cinematic color grading
 • No HDR look
 • No artificial glow
 • No extreme sharpening
 • Very subtle vignette allowed
VISUAL PRIORITY (STRICT ORDER)
1. Product
2. Facial reaction

The viewer should notice the product first, then the human reaction.
The composition should leave clear empty space in the upper-left portion of the frame so headline text can be added later without covering the product or the person.
