Create a high click-through-rate YouTube thumbnail scene for a tech review video using strong foreground/background depth.
The image must look like a real photo captured during filming — spontaneous, natural, and believable. It must not look like a graphic design poster or an AI render.
Colors should appear slightly more vibrant and saturated than normal camera output, while still remaining natural and realistic. The product colors should pop slightly without looking artificially edited.

FRAME COMPOSITION
Design the image with a left-heavy layout and strong visual hierarchy.
The product must appear in the left third of the frame, leaving clear negative space above and to the left of the product so headline text can be added later.
Leave clear breathing room around the product:
• open space above the product
• open space to the left of the product
The composition should intentionally reserve the upper-left quadrant for future thumbnail text.
Nothing important should occupy that area.

LEFT SIDE — FOREGROUND PRODUCT
• Product extremely close to the camera lens
• Occupies roughly 20–25% of frame width
• Clearly the sharpest object in the image
• Held casually in one hand
• Slight natural tilt allowed
• Visible material texture and realistic detail
• Subtle light reflections on surfaces (glass, plastic, metal)
• Product must separate from background using true optical depth of field, not glow effects
• Product must feel physically closer to the viewer than anything else in the frame
• Product should sit slightly below vertical center

Important framing rule:
Leave clean space above the product so arrow graphics or labels could be added later.
The product should visually outweigh the hand, but the hand must still look natural and believable.

RIGHT SIDE — BACKGROUND HUMAN
• Person positioned behind the product on the right side
• Face softly blurred due to shallow depth of field
• The person should appear slightly smaller in frame to maintain safe cropping margins
• Person pointing toward the product
• Expression subtle and believable:
* quiet realization
* mild regret
* thoughtful skepticism
* impressed surprise
Avoid exaggerated YouTube reaction faces.
The human should visually support the product, not compete with it.

CAMERA & REALISM
The camera should feel slightly pulled back so the composition leaves breathing room around the subjects.
The image should feel like it was shot on a 50mm lens at f/1.8–f/2.8 indoors.
• Realistic shallow depth of field
• Natural indoor lighting (window light or soft room light)
• Slight natural shadows
• Natural skin texture with small imperfections
• Slight color vibrancy for visual pop
• No cinematic color grading
• No HDR look
• No artificial glow
• No extreme sharpening
• Very subtle vignette allowed
Lighting should create soft highlights on the product so it visually pops against the background.

COLOR & CONTRAST
Use slightly elevated saturation and contrast to improve thumbnail visibility.
• Product colors should appear rich and vibrant
• Background should be slightly muted so the product stands out
• Avoid dull or gray color palettes
• Maintain natural skin tones

VISUAL PRIORITY (STRICT ORDER)
1️⃣ Product
2️⃣ Facial reaction
The viewer must notice the product instantly, then the human reaction.
The product should visually anchor the entire frame.
